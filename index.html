About Me

Get to know me...
I'm originally from New Jersey, about 20 minutes from the beach and an hour from NYC. I spent summers going down the shore with my family and I’m definitely a beach girl at heart. I can still be found lounging in the sand or swimming in the ocean pretty much any time I can get there! I grew practicing gymnastics, dance, and cheerleading. To this day I still love flipping around, (yes, 30 year olds can still do back flips)! Over the past four years most of my exercise has been focused on Pilates, yoga, and barre. A life goal of mine is to one day become a certified Pilates or yoga instructor – I love the idea of sharing both the mental and physical benefits of my practice with others. I also love cooking, finding ways to make delicious dishes with the fewest and most natural ingredients, attending live music shows, spending time with my family (AKA some of my best friends), and traveling.

Professional life...
So, what about my professional life, you ask? Well, neither professional sun-bather nor avid concert-goer was going to pay the bills, so I explored another life-long passion of mine…

I was interested in fashion from a very young age. When I was a child, I would get home from back-to-school shopping trips and put on fashion shows with my sister, the foyer hallway staged as our runway (of course). I quickly realized modeling was not in the cards for me when I stopped growing at 5’2”, so I decided I’d need to choose a different career if I wanted to be in the fashion industry.

I attended Marist College where I studied Fashion Merchandising and Business. A few weeks after graduation I started my first “real-world” job on the merchandise buying team at Bloomingdale’s. I spent 8 wonderful years in the retail industry, over the course of which I fell in love with testing out new products by bringing them into a store/website, understanding the customers’ reaction through analysis of metrics and KPIs, constantly learning what worked and what didn’t and coming up with strategic solutions on how to solve and fix issues, and adapting and pivoting to meet consumer demand or new trends in strategic and innovative ways –not to mentioned all while working with lots of different people to do so. During my time in retail I learned so much about the industry, business, customer service, problem-solving, working collaboratively across different cross-functional teams, and developed an amazing transferrable skill set of both soft and hard skills. I was also always exposed to aspects of technology as it became more and more prevalent in everyday life and more important and innovative in retail.

The future is bright...
Unfortunately, along with millions of other people across world, I recently lost my job during the Covid-19 pandemic. Right from the get-go I chose to see this not-so-awesome life event as an amazing career change opportunity rather than a failure – I knew this was the start of a great new chapter for me! I’ve always been extremely interested in the way intricate websites, apps, software programs, and in-store/in-warehouse technologies are designed, built, tested, and constantly updated to meet ever-changing business needs and create a better user/customer experience. I’ve also always had an affinity for e-commerce (where I began my career.) That said, put everything together and my most recent decision to enroll in a software development bootcamp as the first step in my career change was a fairly easy one for me. The idea of learning new skills, building something with my own hands (and a keyboard!) and seeing it come to life, all while solving problems and working with many others along the way, is extremely exciting to me.

I can’t wait to embark on my journey to a new career path. I’ve already started learning a bit of code and I’m loving it - check out my first few projects below in the portfolio section!
